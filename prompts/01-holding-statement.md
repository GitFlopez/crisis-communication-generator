# Prompt 1: Initial Holding Statement

## Purpose
Generate a 3-5 sentence holding statement for the first 30-60 minutes of a crisis, before you have all the facts.

## When to Use
- The moment you become aware of a crisis and need to say *something* before you know everything
- Within the first hour — before journalists call, before social media fills the void
- When "no comment" would make things worse but a full statement isn't ready yet

## The Prompt

```
You are a crisis communications expert. Generate an initial holding statement for the following business crisis.

CRISIS DETAILS:
- Company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- What happened: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF THE SITUATION]
- Who is affected: [CUSTOMERS / EMPLOYEES / PUBLIC / OTHER]
- Current status: [ONGOING / CONTAINED / UNDER INVESTIGATION / RESOLVED]
- Your name/title: [NAME, TITLE]

Generate THREE versions of a holding statement (3-5 sentences each):

VERSION 1 — EMPATHETIC TONE
Lead with concern for affected parties. Acknowledge the situation without minimizing it. Signal that you take this seriously. Set a timeline for a full response. Avoid admitting liability or speculating about cause.

VERSION 2 — CORPORATE/FORMAL TONE
Factual, professional language. Acknowledge awareness of the situation. State that an investigation/review is underway. Commit to transparency as facts become available. Appropriate for regulated industries (finance, healthcare, legal).

VERSION 3 — TRANSPARENT/DIRECT TONE
Name what happened plainly (without speculation). Acknowledge impact on affected parties. State what you're doing right now. Give a specific next update time. Appropriate for tech companies and brands with transparency as a core value.

For each version, provide:
- The holding statement (3-5 sentences)
- Best use case (what channel, what situation)
- Tone rating (1-10 on empathy scale, 1-10 on formality scale)

Then provide a DO-NOT-SAY checklist: 10 phrases that would escalate the crisis or create legal liability, with a brief explanation of why each is dangerous.
```

## What You'll Receive
- 3 holding statement versions with tone ratings
- Channel guidance for each version
- 10 phrases to avoid and why

## Example Inputs
- Company: CloudPath | Industry: B2B SaaS | What happened: Unauthorized access to customer email database | Who affected: 12,000 business customers | Status: Contained | Name: Sarah Chen, CEO

## Usage Notes
- Choose the tone that matches your brand voice
- Have legal review before posting — this prompt is a starting point
- Post to: company Twitter/X, LinkedIn, status page, email to affected customers
- Update with full statement within 2-4 hours
