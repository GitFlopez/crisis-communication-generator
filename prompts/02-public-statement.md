# Prompt 2: Full Public Statement

## Purpose
Generate a complete 300-500 word public statement for press release, website, and social media.

## When to Use
- 2-4 hours after the crisis, once you have facts
- When media have picked up the story and you need an official record
- For the company newsroom, press release wire, and pinned social posts

## The Prompt

```
You are a crisis communications expert. Generate a full public statement for the following crisis scenario.

CRISIS DETAILS:
- Company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- What happened: [DETAILED DESCRIPTION — what occurred, when, how it was discovered]
- Who is affected: [SPECIFIC GROUPS — e.g., "customers who logged in between March 1-5", "employees at the Chicago location"]
- Root cause (if known): [CAUSE or "Under investigation"]
- What you've done: [ACTIONS ALREADY TAKEN — e.g., "secured the system", "notified authorities", "set up a hotline"]
- What you're doing next: [COMMITTED NEXT STEPS with timeline]
- Spokesperson name and title: [NAME, TITLE]
- Company values relevant to this crisis: [e.g., "transparency", "customer first", "safety above all"]

Generate a full public statement using the AASS framework:

A — ACKNOWLEDGE: What happened, when, and who is affected. No minimizing.
A — ACCOUNTABILITY: What went wrong, who is responsible (without blame-shifting), and your accountability.
S — SOLUTION: Specific, concrete actions already taken and being taken now.
S — STEPS FORWARD: What you're changing so this doesn't happen again, with timeline.

Format:
1. Opening paragraph (acknowledge + brief accountability)
2. Body paragraph 1 (solution — what you've already done)
3. Body paragraph 2 (steps forward — what changes are coming)
4. Leadership quote (2-3 sentences from spokesperson — human, not corporate)
5. Closing paragraph (contact info, next update commitment, gratitude to affected parties for patience)
6. FAQ section: Answer these 5 questions in 2-3 sentences each:
   - What exactly happened?
   - Why did this happen?
   - Are my [data/safety/money/job] at risk?
   - What are you doing about it?
   - How do I know this won't happen again?

Total length: 350-500 words for main statement, plus FAQ.
Tone: [EMPATHETIC / CORPORATE / TRANSPARENT — specify which]
```

## What You'll Receive
- Full AASS-framework statement (350-500 words)
- Leadership quote
- 5-question FAQ section
- Ready for press release, website newsroom, and email

## Formatting Notes
- Press release: add company boilerplate at bottom
- Social media: extract key sentences for Twitter/LinkedIn posts
- Email to affected customers: add personal greeting and remove press-specific language
