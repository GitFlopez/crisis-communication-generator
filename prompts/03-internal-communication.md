# Prompt 3: Internal Employee Communication

## Purpose
Generate internal communications for employees during and after a crisis — what to say, what not to say, and how to handle customer questions.

## When to Use
- Before or simultaneously with public statement (employees should never hear it from the news)
- When employees are customer-facing and need scripted responses
- Post-resolution to close the loop and rebuild internal trust

## The Prompt

```
You are a crisis communications expert. Generate internal employee communications for the following crisis.

CRISIS DETAILS:
- Company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- What happened: [DESCRIPTION]
- Who is affected externally: [CUSTOMERS / PUBLIC / REGULATORS]
- Employee groups to communicate to: [ALL EMPLOYEES / CUSTOMER-FACING ONLY / LEADERSHIP ONLY / SPECIFY]
- Status: [ONGOING / CONTAINED / RESOLVED]
- Customer-facing roles: [e.g., "support team takes customer calls", "retail staff interact with customers daily", "account managers own client relationships"]
- Resolution timeline: [EXPECTED or "TBD"]
- Sender name/title: [NAME, TITLE]

Generate TWO internal communications:

COMMUNICATION 1 — IMMEDIATE NOTIFICATION (send within 1 hour of crisis detection)
Format:
- Subject line (Slack/email)
- Opening: what happened, factual and calm (2-3 sentences)
- What employees should do right now (3-5 bullet points)
- What to say to customers/clients: approved script (2-3 sentences they can use verbatim)
- What NOT to say: 5 specific phrases to avoid and why
- Who to escalate questions to (name + contact)
- Next update commitment: when employees will hear more

COMMUNICATION 2 — POST-RESOLUTION UPDATE (send within 24 hours of resolution)
Format:
- Subject line
- What happened (brief recap for employees who missed real-time updates)
- What we did to resolve it (concrete actions in past tense)
- What we learned and are changing (3-5 specific process changes)
- Recognition: acknowledge the team's response (specific, not generic)
- Thank you from leadership

SOCIAL MEDIA GUIDANCE SECTION (attach to Communication 1):
- What employees can post publicly (if anything) — specific approved statements
- What to say if friends/family ask about the situation
- How to handle negative comments on personal social accounts
- Response template for customer complaints received via personal channels
- One rule: [STAY SILENT / POST APPROVED STATEMENT ONLY / USE YOUR JUDGMENT with guidelines]
```

## What You'll Receive
- Immediate notification (Slack/email format)
- Post-resolution update
- Social media guidance for employees
- Verbatim customer-facing scripts

## Critical Principle
Employees should never learn about a crisis from the news or social media. Internal communication must go out before — or simultaneously with — the public statement.
