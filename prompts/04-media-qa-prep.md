# Prompt 4: Media Q&A Prep Kit

## Purpose
Generate the 10 hardest journalist questions, approved answers, and a spokesperson briefing package for any media interview during a crisis.

## When to Use
- Before any press interview, even "friendly" ones
- Before posting public comments on high-visibility platforms
- Before investor or board calls during a crisis
- When a journalist requests comment and you have 30 minutes to prepare

## The Prompt

```
You are a crisis communications expert and media trainer. Generate a media Q&A prep kit for the following crisis scenario.

CRISIS DETAILS:
- Company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- What happened: [FULL DESCRIPTION]
- Root cause: [KNOWN CAUSE or "Under investigation"]
- Actions taken: [WHAT YOU'VE DONE]
- Spokesperson: [NAME, TITLE, MEDIA EXPERIENCE LEVEL: none/some/experienced]
- Key messages (what you most want to communicate): [2-3 CORE MESSAGES]
- Topics that are off-limits (legal/regulatory reasons): [LIST or "None"]

Generate the following:

SECTION 1 — THE 10 HARDEST QUESTIONS (with approved answers)
Organize into 5 categories (2 questions each):
1. What happened (factual questions about the incident)
2. Why it happened (root cause, preventability)
3. Who is responsible (accountability, blame)
4. What you're doing about it (remediation, next steps)
5. What guarantees can you give (future prevention, trustworthiness)

For each question:
- The question (as a journalist would actually ask it — adversarial, specific)
- Approved answer (2-4 sentences — truthful, non-evasive, doesn't create new problems)
- Off-limits phrases: 2-3 words/phrases to never say in response to this question
- Bridge opportunity: yes/no — can you pivot from this question to a key message?

SECTION 2 — BRIDGE PHRASE LIBRARY
Generate 10 bridge phrases for redirecting hostile questions back to key messages.
Format: "That's an important question. What I can tell you is..." [variation]
Include: empathy bridges, factual bridges, forward-looking bridges, accountability bridges.

SECTION 3 — APPROVED SOUND BITES (3 total)
One sentence each. Quotable. Accurate. Strong enough to stand alone in a headline.
Each sound bite should carry one of the three key messages.

SECTION 4 — SPOKESPERSON BRIEFING TEMPLATE
- Today's key messages (3 bullets)
- Off-limits topics and why
- "If asked about X, say Y" quick-reference (5 entries)
- Body language notes for video interviews
- How to handle "no comment" without saying "no comment"
- How to correct a journalist's wrong assumption without being combative
```

## What You'll Receive
- 10 adversarial Q&A pairs with off-limits phrases
- Bridge phrase library (10 phrases)
- 3 quotable sound bites
- Spokesperson briefing sheet

## Usage Notes
- Practice the 10 questions out loud before any interview
- The bridge phrase library is the most important tool — master 3-4 of them
- Sound bites should appear verbatim in any formal statement — journalists will use exact quotes
- Have a PR or legal reviewer check the approved answers before media contact
