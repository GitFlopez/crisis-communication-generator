## Overview

Generate professional crisis communication responses that protect your brand when things go wrong. This skill produces four crisis response assets — initial holding statement, full public statement, internal employee communication, and media Q&A prep kit — for any business crisis scenario.

Every business will face a crisis. A product recall, a data breach, a negative viral moment, a leadership scandal, a service outage, a customer injury. How you respond in the first 2 hours determines whether the story ends there or becomes a reputation-destroying news cycle. This skill gives you the words — fast.

## Prompts

### Prompt 1: Initial Holding Statement
Generate a 3-5 sentence holding statement for the first 30-60 minutes of a crisis, before you have all the facts. Acknowledges the situation, signals you're taking it seriously, sets a timeline for a full response, and avoids admitting liability. Includes 3 tone variants (empathetic, corporate, transparent) and a do-not-say checklist of phrases that escalate the crisis.

### Prompt 2: Full Public Statement
Generate a complete public statement (300-500 words) for press release, website, and social media. Follows the AASS framework: Acknowledge (what happened), Accountability (what you're doing about it), Solution (concrete steps being taken), and Steps forward (how this won't happen again). Includes a quote from leadership and a FAQ section answering the 5 questions journalists will ask first.

### Prompt 3: Internal Employee Communication
Generate the internal communication to employees during and after a crisis. Two versions: immediate notification (what happened, what to say to customers, what not to say) and post-resolution update (what we learned, what we're changing, thank you). Includes a "social media guidance" section — what employees should post (if anything), how to handle questions from friends/family, and scripts for customer-facing staff.

### Prompt 4: Media Q&A Prep Kit
Generate the 10 hardest questions a journalist will ask, with approved answers and off-limits phrases for each. Organized by category: what happened, why it happened, who is responsible, what you're doing about it, and what guarantees can you give. Includes a spokesperson briefing template, three approved sound bites, and a "bridge phrase" library for redirecting hostile questions.

## Inputs Required

- **Crisis type** (e.g., data breach, product recall, service outage, negative viral post, leadership scandal, layoff)
- **Company name and industry**
- **What happened** (brief factual description — what you know so far)
- **Who is affected** (customers, employees, public, specific demographics)
- **Current status** (ongoing / contained / resolved)
- **Your name/title** (for leadership quote in public statement)
